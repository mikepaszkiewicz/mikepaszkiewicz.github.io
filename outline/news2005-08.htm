We've Got You Covered

Help Is On The Way For Healthcare Coverage
With estimates of over 40,000,000 un-insured Americans, we have a problem in our country.  The soaring cost of quality health insurance prohibits many businesses from offering insurance to their employees.  As a result, we all end up paying for the uninsured through hospitals, doctors, and insurance companies passing these costs onto us by charging higher premiums to those with insurance or through our tax dollars.  The real solution is to find a way to pay for coverage without pushing the business owner out of the very business that is providing a much-needed job in the community.  Help is on the way, however.  A new concept is here:  Voluntary Limited Benefit Plans.

Imagine one of these scenarios: A 25 year old waiter is suddenly diagnosed with cancer or a 42 year old single mother is diagnosed with diabetes.  In both cases, it is likely that these individuals either did not think they needed coverage or could not afford it.  New voluntary limited medical programs are now available that can be offered at group rates with no contribution required from the employer.  Some of these programs include preventive care, hospitalization benefits, a Prescription card, and even doctor visits.  The premiums are as low as $16.00 a week for an employee, $25.00 a week for an employee and spouse, $23.00 a week for employee and Child or Children and $32.00 a week for Family.  A 10% discount is also offered if the employer contributes at least $35.00 a month toward the coverage.  In addition to the program being voluntary, it is also portable—you can take it with you if you leave your job.

These programs are not intended to replace traditional medical coverage.  Instead, the Limited Medical Plans are meant to fill a void by making medical insurance affordable for those who, in the past, may not have been in a position to purchase a full-blown medical plan.
