We've Got You Covered

Imagine what would happen to your business if you or one of your partners would suddenly pass away.  You may have taken steps to handle the personal situation of providing for your spouse or family - but what about the business.  Can the business survive without you or the revenue you generate or the role you play in the success of the operation?  If you are like most entrepreneurs - the answer is no.  The real answer to this question is to take steps to protect the business through a life insurance product.

Simply put, the business can purchase a life insurance policy on your life (or on the lives of each partner) in order to take care of the debts of the business upon your passing.  At any given time, most businesses have outstanding debt -either inventory they have yet to pay for, outstanding accounts payable, or leased or rented equipment.  A life insurance policy written for an amount to cover those costs can make the process less burdensome on your family, partners and attorneys.  

Another instance in which you may want to purchase life insurance is due to a "Buy/Sell Agreements" in partnerships.  There are many reasons for a Buy/Sell agreement in a partnership.  Buy-sell agreements can be used to prevent unwanted individuals from becoming owners of the business, and they can be used to ensure that the current owner group continues as the future owner group of the business. Buy-sell agreements can provide a market for the business interest that is to be sold and it can provide solutions for various unfavorable or unfortunate contingencies, such as the disability or the divorce of an owner. These agreements are also an invaluable tool for estate planning purposes.  Life insurance can be a valuable tool for "funding" Buy/Sell Agreements.  In other words - to be able to "buy" the shares of the deceased partner, the proceeds of a company purchased life insurance policy can be used to pay for those shares and keep the partnership intact.
